About Me

My name is Tirth Patel, and I'm currently pursuing my Master's in Electrical and Electronics Engineering at California State Polytechnic University, Pomona. I'm passionate about embedded systems, firmware design, and real-time hardware-software integration. My experience spans C, C++, JAVA, Python, Verilog and Assembly, with a solid foundation in GPIO, ADC, SPI, I2C, UART, RTOS fundamentals, Computer Architecture, and other core hardware and embedded engineering skills.

During my undergraduate studies, at the age of 19, I led a team that secured ₹73,000 in government funding for a smart-vehicle startup project, where we developed a safety-focused embedded system from concept to prototype, strengthening my technical and project management skills.

Currently, I'm working as a Systems Engineer in the Northrop Grumman Collaboration Project, contributing to mission planning, model-based system design, and documentation for autonomous systems while ensuring seamless hardware-software coordination. Alongside this, I'm developing an embedded vehicle project called "Bacon Parking Auto-Park", where my team and I are building an autonomous parking car from scratch, integrating sensor-based control, firmware logic, and real-time embedded design.

Previously, I completed two Embedded Software Development internships at Inoweave, where I focused on firmware design, system validation, and real-time interface development, and a Hardware Testing internship at Nexinnovation, where I worked on diagnostics and quality assurance for embedded prototypes.
